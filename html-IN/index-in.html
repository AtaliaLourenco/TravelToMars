The Red Planet

Geologically, Mars and Earth share many common traits, and both are known as terrestrial or rocky planets. Mars has two small moons, and it orbits the sun. The colonization programs on Mars allow us to inhabit and develop life on another planet, reducing the risks of the extinction of human life, and can also evolve our species and make us capable of developing technologies to explore the universe and make contact with other living beings scattered around. across the universe.

Why did we choose Mars?

  • Water on Mars

    It is possible to extract liquid water from ice in the ground, and oxygen can be generated from the carbon dioxide that makes up most of the atmosphere on Mars.

  • Future of Human Race

    Colonies on Mars are able to maintain our population permanently, they provide our species with the ultimate life expectancy. Living on Mars Provides Another Backup Plan for the Future of the Human Race

  • Planetary Exploration

    As on Earth, Mars has a lot of riches in metallic oxides, such as iron, silicon and aluminum, and, with the right technology, it is possible to exploit these resources and develop new technologies.

Excursions to Mars

In addition to the Mars Colonization programs, there is also the possibility of taking excursions to Mars and living the Martian experience on your own. During the excursion, the crew will get to know the routine of the colonists and researchers on Mars, they will also be able to contribute to the research and record everything in videos and photos.
